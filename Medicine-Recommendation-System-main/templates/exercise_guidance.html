Exercise Guidance for Different Age Groups

Under 5 Years

Crawling

Encourage your child to crawl on hands and knees. This exercise improves coordination and strengthens muscles.

Animal Walks

Let your child mimic animal movements like walking like a bear or hopping like a frog. This is a fun way to develop motor skills.

5-12 Years

Jumping Jacks

Have your child jump with arms and legs spread wide, then jump back to a resting position. It boosts heart rate and endurance.

Balancing on One Leg

Get your child to balance on one leg for 10-20 seconds. This exercise helps with balance and focus.

Sit to Stand

Teach your child to sit and stand up without using hands. This improves leg strength and coordination.

13-19 Years

Bodyweight Squat

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, squat down, and then return to standing. This strengthens legs and core.

Push-Ups

Start in a plank position, lower your chest to the ground, and push back up. This exercise strengthens the chest and arms.

Plank

Hold a push-up position with your arms straight and body aligned. This targets the core, arms, and back.

40-59 Years

Brisk Walking

Walk briskly for 30 minutes, aiming for a pace that raises the heart rate. This is great for cardiovascular health.

Yoga

Practice gentle yoga poses like child's pose or downward dog. Yoga improves flexibility and reduces stress.

Dumbbell Exercises

Perform light dumbbell exercises like bicep curls and shoulder presses. This helps with strength and muscle tone.

60+ Years

Walking

Take regular walks to maintain mobility and improve cardiovascular health. Walking is low-impact and accessible for seniors.

Stretching

Do gentle stretches for flexibility, such as neck and shoulder stretches. Stretching reduces stiffness and improves joint health.

Light Yoga

Practice simple yoga poses that promote flexibility and relaxation. This improves balance and reduces stress.
